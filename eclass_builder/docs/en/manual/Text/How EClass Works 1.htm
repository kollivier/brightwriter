Before we start getting into the technical "how to do things" with EClass, I'd like to start by discussing some of the core concepts behind the EClass.Builder program. First we'll need to look at how we see the EClass creation process (really the process for creating documentation or learning modules).



The Creation Process

Whether you are creating a learning module, some form of online documentation, or a web site, the process is very similar. This is why EClass is well-suited for creating all these types of products. In any project, we all perform the following four steps, though some people may perform the steps without even realizing they are doing it!
  1. Design: Define the target audience and their needs, delivery medium (i.e. web site, CD-ROM, etc.), and determine what the product needs. For larger projects, this may include performing surveys and other pre-assessments.
  2. Build: Gather and create content, organize and structure presentation of content, obtain any ncessary copyright permissions, conduct internal testing and reviews.
  3. Deploy: Distribute the content, collect feedback, and provide any additional services (i.e. training sessions, walkthroughs) that go with the product.
  4. Evaluate: Review target audience feedback, review the entire process, define improvements to be made.
There is actually a fifth step as well: Repeat! Do it all over again next time (only better). Notice that these steps are not necessarily linear, and the pie shown above reflects that. The center of the "pie" is your EClass, and the tasks outlined above are constantly taking place to make your EClass a better product.
The eventual goal of the EClass toolkit is to provide a set of tools for each step in this process, although our current product, EClass.Builder, focuses on steps #2-3. So how does EClass help authors with these steps?

Building an EClass
  • Simple interface and small learning curve: EClass.Builder knows a lot about the technical issues involved in making an EClass work so that you don't have to. For example, when you add a file to your EClass, EClass.Builder automatically copies the file into your EClass for you. It also automatically generates the table of contents, and creates "back and next" links on each page for you. The new Link Checker helps ensure that you don't publish an EClass with broken web links either. The result of this is no "broken links" when you publish your EClass to web or CD. That lets you focus on creating the content, not figuring out where to put your files or how to make it work.
  • Accepts many content formats: EClass lets you insert Word Documents, PDFs, PowerPoints, Flash, Windows Media, and other multimedia formats into your EClass. (Though the end user may need a viewer installed on their computer.) This way authors can spend less time trying to get files into the right format, and can use content they've already created as easy as point and click.
  • Easy organization of content: Working in EClass.Builder, you can move, restructure, copy and paste pages wherever you want them to go. This makes it quick and easy to move content around if the structure changes.
  • Themes: Every page has a consistent design, sometimes also called a "Theme", associated with it. This ensures your users do not get confused or lost while navigating your EClass. You can pick a theme from the list of available themes using the Theme Manager, or if you know some HTML, you can create your own. You could also download new themes created by others.
  • Cross-browser Preview: See how the pages of your EClass will look in Internet Explorer, Netscape and Mozilla (Windows only for IE) and discover potential display problems before the user does.
  • Support custom content-formats: Advanced authors or organizations can create their own plugins to support special content formats, such as a quiz or EClass Page. This helps organization link legacy data formats (or special data formats) into an EClass.
Deploying an EClass
  • FTP Publishing: When you want to put your EClass up on the web, just type in your web server, name, password and the directory you'd like to put your EClass, and EClass will do the rest.
  • CD Publishing: EClass.Builder prepares your EClass for CD-ROM delivery by creating an autostart file (for Windows) and making sure every file you need is included within your EClass. Just copy all the files inside your EClass folder to your CD burner software and burn your EClass!
